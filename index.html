About

Hello, welcome to visit my site! 👋

I am a doctoral researcher (Ph.D. candidate) in the research group led by Prof. Dr. Vera Demberg at Saarland University, and I am also affiliated with the ERC-funded project: Individualized Interaction in Discourse. Concurrently, I am a visiting scholar at the University of Edinburgh, working under the guidance of Prof. Dr. Mirella Lapata. My research interests lie primarily in the area of CS ∩ CL ∩ AI.

Prior to joining Saarland University, I earned my Master of Computer Science degree under the supervision of Prof. Dr. Khalil Sima'an at the Institute for Logic, Language and Computation, University of Amsterdam.

Misc.

More about me:
  • I was born in the southernmost city in China: Sanya.
  • Originating from a peasant family, my parents ventured to Sanya City as migrant workers. Throughout my upbringing, they showered me with immeasurable love, for which I will forever remain deeply grateful.
  • During my middle school years, I was somewhat rebellious and authored a novel to critique societal and campus injustices. Over time, I came to understand the complexities of life and the fact that not everything is black and white. This realization led me to make peace with myself and cultivate a more open-minded perspective.
Life pursuit:

I have long aspired to make a meaningful contribution to society, dreaming of a research or work accomplishment that could positively impact countless lives around the world.
